Giovanni Auditore

Year of birth: 1436.

Profession: Banker, adviser.

Giovanni Auditore was one of the most important figures of the fifteenth century
Florentine banking environment. As supervisor of banking houses di Medici
was responsible for the efficient management of the whole the family's financial
empire while Lorenzo was busy ruling. Extending his influence to foreign posts,
he was the first who noticed problems in Lyon and alerted Francesco
Sassettiego - his intervention averted the disaster.

Such great talent was properly rewarded. Giovanni developed a close friendship with Lorenzo de' Medici (Lawrence the Magnificent), becoming one of his closest advisors. The papal support for the Medici bank in 1471 was also the work of Giovanni.

Grateful and reassured, Lorenzo rewarded Giovanni by paying for his palace.




Maria Auditore .....

Year of birth: 1432.

Profession: Writer.

She was born into a powerful family of bankers - the Mozzi. Mary is one
of the most famous historical sources of this period thanks to her
multi-volume a diary that has been translated by many famous people
scientists and is on display at the Uffizi Gallery in Florence.

Thanks to her parents' funds, Maria opened a bakery in the courtyard of the family palazzo, which she transformed into a meeting place for artists. It was there that she met "a man so convincing that I couldn't move or take my eyes off him." That man was Giovanni Auditore.

Maria and Giovanni married in 1450. Maria continued to write her diary for several decades. She describes how she became the patron of many artists, she also writes about the hardships of parenthood, and here her notes end, to the disappointment of all historians.
